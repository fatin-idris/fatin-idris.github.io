Details

This was an individual research for my final year project, where I was responsible for the entire process, from literature review and methodology design to implementation and analysis. I acted as the researcher, developer, and data analyst. My key responsibilities included determined and implementing the image enhancement pipeline using targeted algorithms, including the CycleGAN model. Training the YOLO object detection model on the enhanced datasets. Conducting a comprehensive evaluation of the system's performance using relevant metrics. Documenting the entire process and analyzing the results in a detailed research report.

The main challenge was effectively addressing the visual degradation specific to underwater environments and ensuring the chosen enhancement method was optimized for object detection. This required a deep understanding of multiple machine learning models and careful analysis to prove that a color-corrected image truly improved the detection accuracy. A key part of the process was meticulously preparing and annotating the underwater image dataset to ensure the models were trained on high-quality data.
